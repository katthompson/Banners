Signals collected

The journey starts with collecting audience signals as they interact with communications across all channels. The signals provide specific data on each customer—current and prospective.

Data

keyed CDM

As the signals are collected, the data is cleansed and standardized.

When the system finds multiple records, they are de-duped and combined into a single view.

The data is given a unique and persistent identifier for business accounts and contacts. This is the first step in establishing a single view of your customer.

The customer data-mart is essential to transformational marketing, as it houses all of the customer data. The data is categorized into:

Account – The actual business and attributes about the business. For example: Business Name, Line of Business, Segment, # of employees.

Contacts – The contacts linked to the business and attributes about the contact. For example: Contact Name, Title, Department, Phone, Email Address.

Addresses – The addresses associated with the account and contacts, includes attributes such as Product Eligibility and Competitors at the address location.

Products and Contract Types – AT&T Business products and contract details associated with an account. For example: Product Group, Quantity, Amount, Contract Term and Renewal Date.
